The Moon

Introduction

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It is the fifth largest satellite in the Solar System and the largest among planetary satellites relative to the size of the planet that it orbits.

Physical Characteristics

The Moon is a rocky body, having a diameter of about 3,474 kilometers. It has a surface gravity of about 1/6th that of Earth. The Moon is characterized by its craters, maria, and highlands.

Orbital Properties

The Moon orbits Earth at an average distance of 384,400 kilometers. It takes approximately 27.3 days to complete one orbit around Earth, which is the same amount of time it takes to rotate once on its axis, resulting in the same side always facing Earth.

Exploration

The Moon has been explored by both robotic spacecraft and human missions. The first successful human landing was made by Apollo 11 in 1969, when Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the lunar surface.
